Process

My design process is Human Centered and takes into account the complexity of social organizations, the way they are evaluated, the livability, usability, and sustainability of our relationship with technology, and who benefits from its design.


I use a wide range of tools and methods to conduct research, design, and  collaborate with clients, stakeholders, and owners. I share a sense of pride and ownership with project team members and I use retrospectives to share feedback and improve.

Whether it's product research, market research, or getting to know a project team, Discovery is critical to the UX design process. I like to begin projects by creating user personas, flows, and journey maps. Many discovery activities recur throughout the process, but applying them at the beginning of any project will help generate ideas for research and design further down the road.

1. Discover

During the Ideation stage it's time to dream big or go home! This is when I approach the problem from every angle and generate as many solutions as I can. By measuring and comparing many possible solutions ideas with findings from discovery I can narrow in on the best ones.

2. Ideate

Outlining information architecture and a sitemap is the first steps in translating research and a proposed solution into a functional and usable application. Then, visual and interaction design occur simultaneously as a styleguide is created. With these guidlines in place, I begin creating hi-fi prototypes and detailed specs to communicate design features and decision to clients, stakeholders, owners and leadership.

3. Design

From log in screens to complex data tables and more, usability tests can be used to explore, assess, compare, and validate different designs. I conduct usability tests by asking users to execute tasks in a controlled environment while being observed and recorded. Then I analyze and interpret the data using benchmarks and other criteria before presenting the findings and making recommendations.

4. Evaluate

It's hard to get a design right the first time. That's why every step of the UX process is aimed at incorporating user feedback and refining the design until it is. With every sprint, cycle, or release knowledge is gained and used to refine and improve the user experience. Reflecting on project work also improves my ability to research, design, scope, and plan.

5. Iterate
