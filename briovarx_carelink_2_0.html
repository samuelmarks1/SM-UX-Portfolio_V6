Usability Testing

Due to the compartmentalized nature of users’ task flow, assessment and validation tests were conducted as a combination of formal and informal usability studies.


Informal usability tests were frequently scheduled during regular meetings, where administrative level users would use the prototypes to review and compare various design solutions. For these studies, I participated in experimental design, served as moderator during usability test sessions, and analyzed qualitative feedback.


We conducted formal usability studies with front line associates to assess the usability of frequently used fax queues and patient lookups for new referrals. In these studies I participated in experimental design, attended usability sessions as an observer, and helped identify opportunities to improve speed and efficiency through design.



Design improvements based on research:


  • Optimal UI patterns for discover ability
  • Toast messages to alert agents to contingent system requirements
  • Alert modals To alert agents to contingent system requirements
  • Dialogue modals to accommodate task assignment and resource allocation
  • Scrollable display tables to display frequently referenced data relevant to task completion
  • Progressive disclosure to reduce cognitive load for initial steps in complex workflows with many possible outcomes
  • Accordion features to display details regarding individual case files and workflow statuses

Prototyping and Interaction Design

Leveraging a pre-existing design template, I created a branded Axure component library to rapidly develop data-driven interactive prototypes for each feature and workflow. Design features and workflows were prototyped and updated to a shared URL where the client could review new design and modifications almost daily. Interaction design took place in tandem with rapid iterations of prototyping.

Wireframes and Design

From the architecture outlined in the feature requirements document, I hand-sketched design solutions to validate with users and stakeholders during meetings. After reaching consensus, I moved to Sketch to create lo-fi wireframes of the proposed user interface for the corresponding workflow. By involving users and stakeholders in this process, I was able to quickly identify issues of usability, accessibility, and style and gain buy-in for the most suitable design decisions. Although a style guide was already in place, modifications were made to accommodate functional specifications and user requirements.

Information Architecture

To validate and communicate these requirements, I assisted in the creation of feature requirement documents, which featured detailed Visio diagrams for each compartmentalized portion of the over-arching workflow and the corresponding user requirements and use case scenarios.

User Research

Working closely with managers, developers, and business systems analysts, I met regularly with Executive Project Sponsor, Lead Process Analyst, and other subject matter experts as an ongoing discovery and validation process. During these meetings, I conducted semi-structured interviews and contextual inquiry studies to map workflows and gather user requirements, as well as requirements for business and systems integration.

Briova CareLink 2.0

The BriovaRx Carelink 2.0 Application was an ongoing project that I participated in as UX Designer for 12 months. The particular challenge of this project was to assist intake specialist teams and automate a significant portion of the administrative workflow for this large scale Pharmacy Benefits Manager (PBM). Taking an agile approach, our team tackled individual features and workflows in 2 week sprints alongside development and systems integration.
